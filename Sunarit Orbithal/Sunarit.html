The Problem: A Compositionality Crisis

Despite their success, standard Transformer architectures suffer from foundational flaws that limit their reasoning capabilities and scalability. This "Compositionality Crisis" stems from the computational cost and information bottlenecks inherent in the self-attention mechanism. It is also suspected that unintended pollution is going to be affecting other LLM platform environments as well.   

Computational Complexity: O(n²)

The quadratic scaling of self-attention makes processing long sequences computationally prohibitive, creating a major bottleneck for complex tasks.

The ANDA Solution

ANDA introduces a novel architecture built on two integrated pillars: a bio-inspired processing method and a non-Euclidean computational geometry, designed to solve the compositionality problem from first principles.

The "Breathing" Cycle

The ANDA metabolizes information through a structured, three-stage cycle analogous to cellular respiration. This enables iterative refinement and a form of meta-cognition, allowing the engine to deepen its understanding over multiple cycles.

Phase 1: Inhalation

Acquires and embeds raw input patterns from the environment using a dynamic, hierarchical mechanism to distinguish between background and foreground information.

The "Sphere" Architecture

BSTE abandons flat Euclidean space for a non-Euclidean spherical manifold. This geometry instills powerful inductive biases for unbiased, rotationally-invariant pattern recognition, which is critical for compositional reasoning.

SO(3) Equivariance

Using Spherical Convolutions, the engine can recognize a pattern regardless of its orientation, solving a key failure point of standard attention.

Geodesic Distance

Measures the true shortest path on the sphere's surface, providing a more principled and accurate metric for semantic similarity than cosine distance.
