Beyond the staggering difference in cost, the qualitative performance of the two models diverges significantly, revealing a fundamental difference in their ability to learn and adapt.

Deep Learning: Brittle Specialization

Performance: The DL model performs at a high level on any single task it was last trained on. However, it suffers from **catastrophic forgetting**. When fine-tuned for a new job, its expert knowledge of the previous job is significantly degraded or lost entirely.

Adaptability: It is extremely rigid. It cannot switch between "jobs" without being reconfigured and retrained. Its performance on "combination" jobs is poor unless a massive, expensive training run is conducted on a combined dataset. It cannot dynamically synthesize knowledge.

Panacea Cortex: Resilient Partnership

Performance: The PaCo model also performs at a high level, but its understanding is deeper. Crucially, it **retains all knowledge**. Because the Adaptive Core integrates new information without overwriting old information, PaCo can seamlessly switch between its expert roles.

Adaptability: This is PaCo's defining feature. The Unified Fusion Memory and Deliberative Layer allow it to not only switch between jobs but to *combine* them in novel ways, leading to insights that are impossible for the rigidly trained DL model. It is a true partner that learns and grows with you.
