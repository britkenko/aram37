The Compositionality Crisis

Standard Transformer architectures suffer from foundational flaws that limit their reasoning and scalability. The O(n²) complexity of self-attention creates a computational bottleneck, while information bottlenecks lead to a "Compositionality Crisis," hindering true understanding.

The quadratic scaling of self-attention makes processing long sequences computationally prohibitive.

The ANDA Solution

ANDA introduces a novel architecture built on two integrated pillars: a bio-inspired processing method and a non-Euclidean computational geometry, designed to solve the compositionality problem from first principles.

The "Breathing" Cycle

The ANDA metabolizes information through a structured, three-stage cycle. This enables iterative refinement and a form of meta-cognition, allowing the engine to deepen its understanding over multiple cycles.

The "Sphere" Architecture

BSTE abandons flat Euclidean space for a non-Euclidean spherical manifold. This geometry instills powerful inductive biases for unbiased, rotationally-invariant pattern recognition.

SO(3) Equivariance

Using Spherical Convolutions, the engine can recognize a pattern regardless of its orientation.

Geodesic Distance

Measures the true shortest path on the sphere's surface, providing a more principled metric for semantic similarity.
